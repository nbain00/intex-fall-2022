We want to help you suceed. Here are some help tips to get you started, but remember that it's also important to speak with medical professionals to formulate your health plan:

  1. Avoid sodium! Salt can be damaging. Choose foods with the ‘low sodium’ label. Avoid processed foods as these use lots of sodium. Use spices other than salt to flavor your foods.
  2. Moderate protein! While protein is an essential nutrient, overconsuming it can produce too much weight for your kidneys to handle. Speak with your doctor about what amount of protein is right for you.
  3. Prepare food in a healthy way! Instead of frying, grill. Use less fat. Avoid trans and saturated fats.
  4. Lower Phosphorus! Mayo Clinic’s advice on finding low-phosphorus food.
  5. Balance your potassium levels! NIH’s advice for managing your potassium levels.
